ABC Classification and Slotting Strategy

Student: AKIB ABU SALEH MD | ID: 2217655

1. Empathize

Warehouse staff report congestion and delays in retrieving high-activity SKUs due to an inefficient layout that doesn’t reflect item usage frequency.

2. Define

Problem Statement: “Our warehouse layout does not reflect SKU activity levels, causing unnecessary travel time and pick delays.”

3. Ideate

  • Sort SKUs by pick frequency using Pareto analysis.
  • Classify SKUs into A (high), B (medium), and C (low) activity levels.
  • Redesign layout to bring high-frequency items closer to the dock.

6. Justification & Impact

High-frequency (A-class) SKUs are now located nearest to the dock, reducing walking time and congestion. B-class SKUs are placed around A zones, and C-class SKUs are placed further away, balancing accessibility with priority.

7. Reflection

This project helped me understand how warehouse design impacts operations. Using data-driven decisions like ABC classification improved my ability to design efficient storage layouts that support both performance and practicality.
