Design Thinking Steps

Empathize

Warehouse workers and managers face congestion and inefficiencies due to unoptimized layouts. They need a better strategy to reduce travel distance and improve picking speed.

Define

Problem Statement: Our warehouse layout does not reflect SKU activity levels, leading to extra travel time and delays during order picking.

Ideate

  • Classify SKUs based on pick frequency (ABC classification)
  • Design new zones prioritizing accessibility
  • Apply Pareto analysis to determine which SKUs should be closer to the dock

Prototype

Using the 5x6 warehouse grid and pick frequency data, SKUs were classified:

  • A items (Top 20% of cumulative picks) were placed closest to the dock
  • B items (Next 30%) were placed in mid-access areas
  • C items (Remaining 50%) were stored farthest away or in less accessible zones

Test

By comparing the heatmap of the initial and optimized layouts, we observed that most of the high-frequency items were now closer to the dock at (4,3), minimizing travel distance and expected congestion.

Results & Justification

I performed a Pareto analysis and computed the cumulative pick frequency percentages. Based on this, I assigned ABC categories and rearranged SKU positions accordingly, avoiding the blocked cell at (2,4). This led to a more efficient layout.
