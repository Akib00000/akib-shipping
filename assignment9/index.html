1. Empathize

Our stakeholders are warehouse pickers and logistics managers who face frequent congestion and delays in retrieving high-activity items because the layout doesn't match SKU activity levels.

2. Define

Problem Statement: “Our warehouse layout does not reflect SKU activity levels, causing unnecessary travel time and pick delays.”

3. Ideate

  • Sort SKUs by pick frequency.
  • Apply Pareto analysis and classify items into A, B, and C classes.
  • Redesign layout so A items are nearest to the dock at (4,3).

6. Justification & Impact

The revised layout brings all A-class SKUs within close proximity to the dock at (4,3), drastically reducing walking distance and time. B-class SKUs are positioned around A zones, allowing reasonable access. C-class SKUs are stored further from the dock, where frequent access is not necessary.
