🎯 Selected Logistics Stage: Reverse Logistics

🔍 Research Phase

Initial Problem:
Reverse logistics is a part of supply chain management I didn’t understand much before. It’s all about managing returned goods, recycling, repairs, and the flow back to the supplier. I found out that this process can be very costly and complicated, especially for e-commerce companies.

Empathize:

  • Problem Overview: Companies face huge issues managing returns.
  • Common Causes: Manual processing, unclear product handling strategy, disconnected systems.
  • Examples: Fashion brands like Zara struggle with fast returns. Apple recycles but has global challenges managing returns.
  • Impact: High costs, wasted resources, environmental harm.

🧪 Learning Activity Design

Define – Problem Statement:
"Students need to understand how reverse logistics impacts operations and the environment, and learn how to make smart decisions about handling returned products."

Ideate – Activity Idea:
Reverse Logistics Game – an interactive simulation where students act as logistics managers.

  • Players get different types of returned items.
  • Decide whether to recycle, refurbish, resell, or discard.
  • Points based on cost-saving and environmental impact.
  • Realistic constraints like space and budget.

Student Task: Analyze and choose handling methods for returns to minimize cost and maximize sustainability.

🎓 Why This Helps

This game helps students (like me) see how complex reverse logistics is and trains us to make smart decisions. It connects cost-efficiency with environmental thinking in a fun way.
